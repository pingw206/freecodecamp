Marie Curie

The first woman to win a Nobel Prize

Marie Curie, with her husband Pierre and daughter Irene, pictured in 1899. All three were Nobel prize winners, with Marie winning it twice.

Here's a time line of Marie Curie's life:
  • 1867 - Marie Sklodowska is born in Warsaw, Poland
  • 1891 - Marie Sklodowska registers as a student at the Sorbonne
  • 1895 - Marie Sklodowska marries Pierre Curie
  • 1897 - Irene Curie is born
  • 1898 - Marie Curie introduces the term radioactivity in a published article
  • 1898 - Pierre and Marie Curie announce the discovery of polonium
  • 1898 - Pierre Curie, Marie Curie, and Gustave Bemont announce the discovery of radium
  • 1903 - Henri Becquerel, Pierre Curie, and Marie Curie share the Nobel Prize for physics for their work on radioactivity
  • 1904 - Eve Curie is born
  • 1906 - Marie Curie becomes a widow when Pierre Curie dies in an accident
  • 1906 - Marie Curie becomes the first women professor at the Sorbonne
  • 1911 - Marie Curie is awarded the Nobel Prize for chemistry for discovering radium and polonium and for isolating radium, thus becoming the first person to receive two Nobel Prizes
  • 1914 - Construction of the Radium Institute is completed just after the outbreak of World War 1
  • 1914-1919 - Marie Curie runs a radiology service and introduces radon therapy to France.
  • 1921 - Marie Curie travels to the United States to receive a gram of radium donated by the American people to the Radium Institute.
  • 1934 - Irene and Frederic Joliot-Curie announce their discovery of artificial radioactivity
  • 1934 - Marie Curie dies, at the age of 66, of aplastic anemia
  • 1995 - Marie Curie is reburied in the Pantheon, France’s national mausoleum, becoming the first women accorded that honor on her own merit
